Kenshi Yonezu

In 2012, he transitioned to releasing music under his real name, stepping up to the microphone himself with his debut album Diorama. Yonezu is a remarkably versatile, self-contained artist; beyond writing and composing his music, he frequently orchestrates the music videos, dances, and illustrates his own album covers. His mainstream breakthrough reached historic heights with the 2018 smash single "Lemon", written as the theme song for the TV drama Unnatural. The hauntingly beautiful track became the first song by a Japanese artist to surpass 100 million views on YouTube and went on to break multiple Billboard Japan records. Yonezu is also deeply celebrated for his anime soundtracks, contributing massive hits such as "Peace Sign" (My Hero Academia), "KICK BACK" (Chainsaw Man), which made history as the first Japanese-language song to be certified Gold by the RIAA, and "Spinning Globe" for Hayao Miyazaki’s Oscar-winning Studio Ghibli film, The Boy and the Heron. Known for his introspective lyrics, distinctive vocal delivery, and genre-blending production, Yonezu remains a defining creative force in global Japanese pop culture.
